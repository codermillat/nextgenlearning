The decision to study overseas is a monumental one, weighing considerations of educational standards, financial commitment, and future career prospects. In this context, India has emerged as a premier and highly practical choice, providing world-class education within a landscape that feels like a home away from home.

Several key factors make India a uniquely attractive educational hub for students from Bangladesh:

This ensures that degrees awarded are not only valued in India but also hold international credibility for further studies or careers across the globe.
* Cultural Proximity & Seamless Adaptation: The transition to a new country is significantly eased by the deep-rooted cultural, linguistic, and culinary similarities between Bangladesh and India. This shared heritage mitigates the culture shock often experienced in Western countries, allowing students to adapt quickly and focus on their academic goals.

This affordability, combined with high-quality education and strong career outcomes, represents an exceptional return on investment.
* Economic Synergy & Career Prospects: As one of the world's fastest-growing economies, India ensures that its academic curricula are modern, relevant, and aligned with current industry demands. This provides Bangladeshi students with an education that equips them with the skills to thrive in evolving job markets, both in India and back home.

3.0 Academic Opportunities: Programs and Eligibility for Bangladeshi Students

Sharda University provides a vast landscape of academic choices, offering over 135+ programs across a wide spectrum of disciplines. This breadth ensures that students can find a course that aligns perfectly with their career aspirations. This section details the programs most relevant to Bangladeshi students and outlines the specific eligibility criteria required for admission.

3.1 Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech)

This flagship 4-year program offers a wide range of engineering disciplines, from foundational branches to cutting-edge specializations.

* Core Branches: Computer Science & Engineering (CSE), Electronics & Communication Engineering (ECE), Mechanical Engineering.
* In-demand Specializations: AI & Machine Learning, Blockchain, Data Science.
* Eligibility: HSC (Science) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) is mandatory.

3.2 Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)

A versatile 3-year degree suitable for students from any academic background, the BBA program prepares graduates for a dynamic corporate world.

* Specializations: International Business, Marketing Management, Finance & Accounting (with ACCA, UK integration).
* Eligibility: HSC from any stream (Science, Commerce, or Arts) is accepted.

3.3 B.Sc. Nursing

This 4-year professional program is designed to meet the high global demand for qualified healthcare professionals and offers strong career prospects.

* Eligibility: HSC (Science) with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) is required.

3.4 Medical & Dental Programs (MBBS/BDS)

It is critical for aspiring medical and dental students from Bangladesh to understand the stringent national regulations governing these programs in India. Admission is NOT based on HSC results. Qualification in the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET), a highly competitive exam conducted in India, is mandatory for all applicants, including international students. After qualifying NEET, students must participate in a multi-step state counseling process conducted by DGME, U.P. to secure a seat. This pathway is exceptionally competitive and requires careful planning and preparation separate from the standard university application process. To be clear, this is an exceptionally difficult path for international students due to the sheer volume of domestic competition for a limited number of seats. Prospective applicants must realistically assess their ability to score in the highest percentiles of the NEET exam against millions of Indian aspirants.

4.1 Scholarships for Bangladesh

Sharda University offers a merit-based scholarship scheme that provides substantial financial relief to students from Bangladesh.

* 50% Scholarship on Tuition Fee: Awarded to students with a GPA of 3.5 to 5.0 in their qualifying examination.
* 20% Scholarship on Tuition Fee: Awarded to students with a GPA of 3.0 to 3.4.

The premier 50% scholarship is applicable to a wide range of popular programs, including B.Tech, BBA, MBA, and BCA. For certain professional programs, specific scholarship policies apply:

* B.Sc. Nursing: A flat 25% scholarship is awarded every year on the tuition fee.
* Other Programs: For most other programs, a flat 20% scholarship is awarded every year.

Programs with NO Scholarship

It is crucial to note that no scholarships are offered for the following high-demand professional courses:

* Pharmacy (B.Pharm, D.Pharm)
* M.Sc. Nursing
* Master of Physiotherapy (MPT)
* BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery)
* MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine & Bachelor of Surgery)

Scholarship Continuation Policy

The continuation of the GPA-based scholarship from the second year onwards is contingent on strong academic performance. To retain the scholarship, a student must pass all subjects without any backlog and maintain a minimum of 75% attendance. Failure to meet these conditions will result in the loss of the scholarship for subsequent years, which could unexpectedly double the tuition fee. Students must honestly evaluate their academic discipline and consistency before factoring this high-value scholarship into their long-term financial plan.

5.0 The Student Journey: Application, Visa, and University Support

Sharda University has developed a streamlined and supportive process for international students, managed by its dedicated International Relations Division. This section provides a step-by-step guide for applicants, walking them through the entire journey from initial application to their arrival in India.

Step-by-Step Application Process

1. Online Application: The process begins by completing the application form on the university's international portal and uploading scanned copies of academic documents (SSC/HSC mark sheets and certificates) and a valid passport.
2. Eligibility Screening: The university's admissions team verifies the submitted documents to confirm eligibility. This is often followed by a personal interview with a faculty member, typically conducted online or via telephone.
3. Provisional Offer Letter: Upon successful screening and interview, a Provisional Offer Letter is sent to the selected candidate's email address.
4. Acceptance & Payment: To secure the seat, the student must formally accept the offer and pay an initial advance fee as instructed in the offer letter.
5. Final Letters for Visa: Once the payment is confirmed, the university issues the two most critical documents for the visa application: the final Admission Letter and the Visa Invitation Letter. These are essential for applying for an Indian Student Visa in Bangladesh.

The Role of the International Relations Division (IRD)

The IRD is the central point of contact for all international students. Its role extends far beyond admissions, providing comprehensive support to ensure a smooth transition. The IRD assists with airport pickup, hostel allotment, and crucial guidance with the mandatory Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO) process, which all foreign students must complete upon arrival in India.

9.0 Essential Handbook: Visa, Finances, and Regulations

A smooth and successful transition to studying in India requires a clear understanding of the official procedures. This section serves as a crucial handbook covering the non-negotiable legal and financial steps that every Bangladeshi student must navigate.

9.1 Navigating the Indian Student Visa

Securing an Indian student visa is a mandatory prerequisite. The process is straightforward but requires meticulous documentation.

1. Before applying, you must have the final Admission Letter and the Bonafide Visa Invitation Letter issued by Sharda University.
2. Gather all required documents, including your passport, academic credentials, and the university letters. A critical requirement is the Proof of Financial Soundness, such as a bank statement or foreign currency endorsement.
3. Submit the completed application form and all supporting documents at an Indian Visa Application Centre (IVAC) in Bangladesh.

9.2 Transferring Fees from Bangladesh

The transfer of funds from Bangladesh for educational purposes is regulated by Bangladesh Bank and must be done through official channels.

* Crucially, you must use the official "Student File" system governed by Bangladesh Bank. This involves opening a file at an Authorized Dealer (AD) bank in Bangladesh.
* You must submit the university's official admission letter and fee invoice to the bank. The bank will then directly remit the tuition fees to the university's account in India.
* Sharda University Bank Details (Example):
  * Beneficiary: Sharda University
  * Bank: ICICI Bank, Greater Noida, India
  * SWIFT Code: ICICINBBCTS

9.3 Rules on Part-Time Work

It is vital for students to understand the regulations regarding employment while studying in India for accurate financial planning.

* The Government of India strictly prohibits foreign nationals on a student visa from undertaking any form of part-time or full-time employment. This rule is non-negotiable.
* The only exception is for internships that are a formal, mandatory, and credited part of the academic curriculum. Any work undertaken for experience outside the curriculum, whether paid or unpaid, is not permitted.
